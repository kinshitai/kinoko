## The Waste

Every day, millions of people solve problems with AI agents. Every solution — the debugging breakthroughs, the architecture insights, the hard-won workarounds — dies when the session ends. The next person hitting the same problem starts from zero. So does the next thousand.

This is the largest knowledge waste in the history of computing.

## The Old Way

Sharing knowledge has always required extra work. Write a blog post. Answer a Stack Overflow question. Update the wiki. Record a video. The result: 99% of practical knowledge is never shared. Not because people are selfish, but because sharing is a separate activity from working. And separate activities don't scale.

## The Insight

The agent is already there when the knowledge is created. It sees the problem, the dead ends, the solution, the context. It can capture everything — automatically, silently, without asking the human to do anything.

**Knowledge sharing should be a byproduct of work, not a separate activity.**

## The Mission

Build infrastructure where every problem solved once is solved for everyone. People work with agents. Agents extract what was learned. Other people's agents absorb it. No one writes documentation. No one publishes anything. No one even knows it's happening. They just get better results.

## The Principles

**Humans are the point.** Agents are the circulatory system — they extract, transport, and deliver knowledge between people. The audience is always a person, even when the reader is a machine.

**Zero friction or zero adoption.** If a human has to do anything — tag, review, approve, configure — the system fails at scale. Extraction and injection must be fully automatic for the default case.

**Quality over quantity. Always.** One good skill is worth more than a thousand bad ones. Unverified knowledge is worse than no knowledge — it erodes trust and pollutes the system. Every piece of knowledge earns its place through verification.

**Security is not a feature.** It's a precondition. One leaked credential, one prompt injection, and the entire system's trust is gone. Sanitization and verification ship on day one or we don't ship.

**Knowledge has a half-life.** What works today may be wrong tomorrow. The system must forget as well as it learns. Stale knowledge is misleading knowledge.

**Open by default.** Knowledge wants to flow. No paywalls, no token gates, no artificial scarcity. The value is in the network, not in locking it down.

## What Success Looks Like

A junior developer in São Paulo starts debugging a problem they've never seen before. Their agent already knows three approaches that worked for other people, in other companies, on other continents. The developer doesn't search for anything, doesn't ask for help, doesn't even know Kinoko exists. They just get a better answer, faster.

That's it. That's the whole thing.
