# Dead Ends & Weak Analogies

## 2026-02-15
- Immune system self/non-self for extraction: interesting but requires training data we don't have yet. Marked as "stretch" — revisit when we have labeled data.
- Single-metric quality scoring: confirmed bad idea across every field studied. Don't go there.

## 2026-02-15 — Post-Feedback Learning
- **Biology analogies without substance**: Quorum sensing, immune system voting just reduce to "use multiple classifiers and vote" — the analogy adds flavor but no implementation insight. Focus on the concrete mechanisms, not the biological metaphor.
- **Forest fire ecology too obvious**: "Delete old stuff, protect important stuff" isn't profound. The useful insight was about different persistence rules for different knowledge types, which came through better in the radioactive decay model.
- **Spreading too thin**: 15 analogies is too many. Better to go deep on 2-3 ideas with concrete implementations than to survey many fields superficially.
- **"Concrete means implementable"**: Not "build a taxonomy" but "here is the draft taxonomy." Not "use dimensions" but "here are the 7 dimensions with scoring criteria."
